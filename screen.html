    Song Editor — User Guide

    Turn a recording into a playable chart: line a grid up to the music, place the notes, mark the tempo and sections, and build a .feedpak. Keys below are the default FeedBack profile — press ? for the full, profile-aware shortcut list.

    1 · Start a project

    • From a recording — load audio and you get an empty timeline over the waveform. Set the tempo grid (§6), then place notes.
    • From an import — File ▸ Import reads Guitar Pro (GP3–GP8), MIDI (with its tempo map), community arrangement XML, GoPlayAlong sync sidecars, and existing .feedpak files. All normalize into one model, so editing is the same afterward.

    Nothing touches your library until you Build (§9). Save early with Ctrl+S.

    2 · The workspace

    • Menu bar — every command, grouped by what it acts on. Follows your shortcut profile; greys out what doesn't apply.
    • Timeline canvas — waveform, beat grid, and notes. Click here to edit; zoom and scroll to navigate.
    • Transport bar — play/stop, clock, loop, count-in, metronome, follow-playhead.
    • Inspector — details for the current selection (a note's fret/techniques, a barline's BPM, a section's name).
    • Companion strips — a fretboard strip (candidate positions — click to assign) for fretted tracks, a drum pad strip for drums.
    • Mixer (Shift+C) — per-track volume / mute / solo.

    Press ? for the searchable shortcut panel, or Ctrl+K for the command palette.

    3 · Play & navigate

    • Space plays/stops. Follow playhead (Shift+L) scrolls with playback.
    • Loop A/B (Alt+B) compares the recording against your guide. Count-in adds a bar of clicks before the entry.
    • Guide claps (C) tick the charted notes; the metronome ticks the beat — use them to hear whether notes land where the grid says.
    • Move: PgUp/PgDn beat to beat; Alt+←/→ note to note. Set bookmarks Shift+Alt+1…9, jump with Alt+1…9.

    4 · Edit notes

    Click a note to select; drag a box for several. Then:

    • Fret — F to edit, or type 0–9 (Shift+0 for 10; Ctrl++/- to nudge).
    • String — ↑/↓ move between strings; Shift+↑/↓ move keeping the pitch (cycles fretting positions in the roll).
    • Timing — drag along the timeline. With snap (G to toggle, ,/. for resolution) notes land on the grid; switch snap to target audio onsets when matching a loose take.
    • Sustain — drag the tail (or Note ▸ Shorten/Lengthen sustain; [/] in the EOF profile). Duplicate to next position Ctrl+D; select matching Ctrl+L; resnap Shift+R.

    Techniques toggle on the selection — hammer-on H, pull-off P, bend B, slide S, palm-mute M, vibrato V, harmonics N/Shift+N, accent A, mute X. The rest are under Note ▸ Techniques with their keys shown.

    The playability and drum-limb lints flag awkward or impossible fingerings — they advise, never block or auto-change your chart.

    5 · Tracks

    A song holds several tracks (lead, rhythm, bass, keys, drums). Each gets the right view automatically — string lanes, piano roll, or drum grid. Switch with the track selector; Tracks overview (Shift+A) stacks them all. Rename / reorder from the Track menu; add a track by importing into it.

    6 · Tempo mapping — line the grid up

    The grid is beat-primary: a note remembers its bar-and-beat and derives its clock time from where the barlines sit — move a barline and the notes ride along. The ruler is authoritative: you fit bars to the fixed recording, never the reverse. Three ways, coarse to fine:

    1. Sync tempo to audio (Tempo/Grid menu) — detects the BPM and scales the whole grid to match. A good first pass on a steady take.
    2. Set a constant BPM — type into the BPM box for a single-tempo song.
    3. Tempo Map mode (T) — the precise tool. Drag a barline onto its downbeat and neighbours re-space to fit. Inside it: G suggest a fit from the audio's onsets (click a ghost to accept), Shift+B tap tempo, B set BPM, M metric modulation, I mark a barline, Del delete one, Shift+T time signature. Beat-lock a hand-verified barline so later auto-fits leave its time alone (your manual edits are always kept).

    The Offset box shifts the whole chart in time against the recording — for when the notes are right relative to each other but the chart sits a few ms early or late.

    Sync, BPM-rescale, and Offset move every track at once and are undoable — experiment freely.

    7 · Drums & 8 · Structure

    • Drums — a piece-lane grid (rows = kit pieces, columns = grid positions). Click to place; the pad strip monitors a MIDI e-kit. Row density (Full / Compact) is in the Track menu.
    • Add menu — sections Shift+M, phrases Shift+P, hand anchors Shift+F, handshapes Ctrl+H, tone changes Ctrl+Shift+T.

    9 · Save & build

    Ctrl+S saves your session. Build assembles the finished .feedpak into your library — the only step that changes what the rest of the app sees. Build once the grid fits, the notes are placed, and guide-claps-against-the-recording sounds right.

    Undo/redo is the safety net — every edit, including tempo moves, offset, and imports, is undoable (Ctrl+Z / Ctrl+Y). If something looks wrong, undo it.

    More help: ? shortcut panel · Ctrl+K command palette. Full written copy: docs/USER-GUIDE.md in the plugin repo.

    Strings (extended range)

    Guitar arrangements support 6–8 strings (added low: B, then F#); bass 4–6 (low B, then high C). Each add/remove button is enabled only at the end that matches the instrument's extended-range layout. To retune a string — drop, open, or a re-entrant/octave setup — type its offset (semitones from the lane's standard pitch) directly in the row; that never changes the string count. Going above 6/4 strings requires saving as Sloppak.
